Why is sleep important

Sleep is crucial for overall health and well-being, supporting physical recovery, brain function, and mental health, while also impacting metabolism, immune function, and mood.

Sleep deficiency is linked to many chronic health problems, including heart disease, kidney disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, stroke, obesity, and depression. Sleep deficiency is also linked to a higher chance of injury in adults, teens, and children.

How to tell if you're sleep deprived?

You might be sleep-deprived if you experience persistent fatigue, difficulty concentrating, mood swings, and reduced physical strength, along with other symptoms like frequent yawning and needing naps during the day.
